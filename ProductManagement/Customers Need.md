Understanding customers need means that you understand what they really need and not what they are asking for. 
Often time a customer express his need in the form of existing solution. The true need is not in the solution is in the problem the solution try to solve for. 
Example: when a mother ask her kid "are you hungry" the kid may response with "I am not sure, but I want to have Pizza". In that example the true need is to eat something while the kid express his need in offering a specific solution in this case a Pizza.
Why discovering the true need is so important? because it allow you to solve the problem in a different ways, with new or other tools than what your customer currently think of. In our example the Mother can offer a health food instead of a Pizza.
